Why speed training?

The ACTIVE study (Advanced Cognitive Training for Independent and Vital Elderly) followed 2,832 older adults for over 10 years. Participants who completed speed-of-processing training — exercises that require identifying and responding to visual information under time pressure — showed a 29% reduced risk of dementia compared to the control group.

Critically, this was the only type of cognitive training (out of memory, reasoning, and speed) that produced a statistically significant reduction in dementia incidence. The benefit was dose-dependent: more sessions led to greater protection.

Edwards et al. (2017), Alzheimer's & Dementia; Rebok et al. (2014), J. Am. Geriatrics Soc.; Ball et al. (2002), JAMA.
